# Claims

## Supported Claims

- The repository implements a real local fixture-physics benchmark.
- Hidden fixtures can be probed through force, displacement, resistance, release, recoil, and torque observations.
- A simple prototype system-identification baseline is very strong in this benchmark.
- The proposed physics-structured latent fixture inference ablations matter internally, but the full method does not beat the strongest baseline.

## Unsupported Claims

- Do not claim ICLR-main readiness.
- Do not claim state-of-the-art fixture inference.
- Do not claim real-robot validation.
- Do not claim latent fixture inference beats prototype system identification.
- Do not claim the method reduces damage or repeated failures relative to the strongest baseline.

## Terminal Claim

Paper 77 is a useful negative result: structured latent-fixture heuristics are not enough when a simple probe-prototype classifier identifies the fixture family more reliably.
